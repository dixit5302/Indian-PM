Indian Prime Ministers

Narendra Modi

About:

Narendra Modi is the first prime minister of India. He was born after India’s independence. He came into power as the Prime Minister of this nation on 26 May 2014. Then he was re-elected for the second time on 30 May 2019. He belongs to the Bharatiya Janta Party (BJP).

Achievements:

Swachh Bharat Abhiyan: A nationwide cleanliness campaign aimed at improving sanitation and hygiene, addressing a critical public health issue. Digital India: A push for technology-driven governance to bridge the digital divide, empower citizens, and enhance transparency.

Manmohan Singh

About:

Manmohan Singh (Punjabi; born 26 September 1932) is an Indian retired politician, economist, academician and bureaucrat who served as the 13th Prime Minister of India from 2004 to 2014. He is the third longest-serving prime minister after Jawaharlal Nehru and Indira Gandhi

Achievements:

Singh devalued the rupee, lowered taxes, privatized state-run industries, and encouraged foreign investment, reforms that helped transform the country’s economy and spark an economic boom. A member of the Indian National Congress, he joined the Rajya Sabha (upper chamber of Parliament) in 1991.

PV Narasimha Rao

About:

Pamulaparthi Venkata Narasimha Rao (28 June 1921 – 23 December 2004), popularly known as P. V. Narasimha Rao, was an Indian lawyer, statesman and politician who served as the 9th prime minister of India from 1991 to 1996. He was the first person from South India and second person from non-hindi speaking background to be the prime minister.

Achievements:

Steering India through the 1991 economic crisis Completing a tenure with a minority government Establishing diplomatic relations with Israel Starting India's Look East policy Rekindling India's nuclear programme Defeating the 1994 United Nations resolution against India Effectively handling and crushing insurgency in Punjab.
